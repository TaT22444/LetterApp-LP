Most of the time, texting works fine. But sometimes — a birthday, a thank-you, or just a moment that matters a little more — I wish I could put a bit more care into what I send. That's why I'm building Chocoleta. The name comes from Chocolate, Letter, and "chokotto" — a Japanese word meaning "just a little bit." In Japan, people give chocolate on Valentine's Day — sometimes casually to friends or coworkers, sometimes with more feeling to someone special. I liked that image of wrapping a small thought and handing it to someone, and used it as a motif. The app isn't about writing long heartfelt letters. It's about making your everyday messages feel a little nicer — just a little upgrade to what you already do. In the app, you write on stationery, attach photos, pick a font and color, seal the envelope with an emoji stamp, and send it with a slingshot gesture. The person who receives it gets a 3D envelope they can tap to open. It's still in development and I'm working on it solo, so there's a lot to figure out.
